Choosing Your Dorm:

A.T.E.N.E.O. Guidelines

  • A

    Amenities: Look for dormitories with essential amenities such as study areas, recreational facilities, laundry services, and dining options.

  • T

    Transportation: Consider the proximity of the dormitory to public transportation or campus shuttle services for convenience in commuting to classes and other activities.

  • E

    Environment: Assess the overall atmosphere and surroundings of the dormitory, including cleanliness, safety, and noise levels.

  • N

    Neighborhood: Explore the neighborhood surrounding the dormitory to ensure it's safe, convenient, and offers access to essential services like groceries, pharmacies, and healthcare facilities.

  • E

    Expenses: Evaluate the cost of living in the dormitory, including rent, utilities, meal plans, and any additional fees, to ensure it fits within your budget.

  • O

    Ownership: Research the ownership and management of the dormitory to ensure it’s reputable and responsible, providing reliable services and addressing any issues promptly.
